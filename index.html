Your Guide to Geneva, Switzerland

About the city

The real home of Lake Geneva (no, not the one in Wisconsin), Geneva is the southernmost city in Switzerland. It is framed by the Alps and Jura mountains, and is host to various major international organizations like the UN and Red Cross. Earning its reputation as the "capital of peace", this multicultural city combines old-town charm with a modern city vibe seemlessly.


Welcome to the city where mornings begin with the glow of the sunlight reflecting off the soft water of the lake, and where nights are graced with the soft bustle of this compact city.


Ready to begin your very own adventure?


Why is it so special?

There's something uniquely comforting about Geneva that makes it feel both small and global at the same time. From its physical landmarks to its culture and traditions, the city has accrued a variety of special traits.

Here are a few of the most notable ones:

  • Jet D'eau: the iconic water fountain located in the heart of Lake Geneva
  • Floral Clock: an extravagant clock made out of seasonal flowers & rearranged for every season
  • Watches & Watchmaking: Geneva is the watchmaking capital of Switzerland (and dare I say, the world?)
  • CERN: the European Organization for Nuclear Research (wow?????)

    • You can learn more about CERN by clicking here!


  • Palais des Nations: an inspiring location where the European headquarters for the United Nations resides

Did you know?

The Jet D'Eau shoots up to 140 meters (460 feet) high, making it one of the tallest water fountains in the world!
Lake Geneva is called Lac Léman in French
Geneva has two rivers, the Arve and the Rhône, which meet towards the city center and create a beautiful blend of the two very different colored-waters
You can see both the Alps and the Jura mountains from the lake
CERN in Geneva is where the World Wide Web was invented in 1989

Where should you begin?


It may all seem like too much and too foreign to anyone wanting to visit, but I assure you Geneva is one of the most welcoming cities out there!

If you too are feeling a tad overwhelmed and do not feel like deep-diving into TripAdvisor in order to curate the trip of your dreams, here is a begginer's guide to Geneva.


Geneva in a day: What to do & Where to go

  1. Coffee & breakfast by the lake

    2. Grab a coffee and a pastry (I personally recommend croissants) from any of the nearby cafes in Eaux-Vives, and head to the Jardin Anglais (English Garden) area for the best view.


  2. See the Jet D'Eau

    3. After your delicious breakfast, walk by the lakeside towards the Jet D'Eau. You can go up really close to it!


  3. Old Town visit

    4. Continue your treck into the city-centre and keep walking uphill to reach the Old Town. Once you get there, make sure to stop by the St. Pierre Cathedral!


  4. Parc des Bastions & the Reformation Wall

    5. From the Old Town, make your way to the Parc des Bastions either by foot or by taking a bus. There, you can check out the Reformation Wall or sit on the grass to take a moment to soak it all in!


  5. Lunch!

    6. Since you'll be in Parc by Bastions by this point, you can either walk or take the tram to any spots around Plainpalais! The food around there is delishhhhh.


  6. Place du Bourg-de-Four

    7. From Bastions, you can take the stairs up to Place du Bourg-de-Four, which is the oldest square in Geneva. Explore its hidden alleys, boutiques, small galeries, and antiques shops.


  7. Carouge for the rest of the night

    8. Take a tram or a bus towards the Carouge district, the most Italian feeling area in Geneva. Carouge has countless hidden gems, go discover them and find a place to sit down for dinner & enjoy the early evening vibes.


  8. If you're not ready to end the day yet...

    9. This one is only for the party animals out there: head to either Eaux-Vives or back to Plainpalais to find copious amounts of bars to choose from. Pick one and enjoy the night!

Some things to keep in mind:

  • The primary spoken language is French, but don't fret!

    • Since Geneva is such a culturally diverse city, everyone there speaks English too!


  • Get ready to walk. A lot.

    • One of the things that makes Geneva (and most other European cities) so special is how walkable it is, so get ready to get your walking game on!


  • Use the public transportation to get around

    • Geneva's public transport system, operated by TPG, is one of the best in the world. Use it!


  • Be respectful of the people and the environment

    • The citizens are used to tourists, but that doesn't mean they appreciate unnecessarily high volume...


  • This is the most important one: Enjoy!

    • Geneva is a wonderful place to visit, cherish your time there!
